Australian Consumer Law

Product safety in Australia is governed by the Australian Consumer Law (ACL). The ACL is contained in a schedule to the Competition and Consumer Act 2010 (CCA). Relevant provisions can also be found in state and territory fair trading legislation.

Product safety provisions

The ACL product safety provisions set out how the Australian Government and the state and territory governments can regulate consumer goods and product-related services to address safety hazards. These can include:
  1. imposing mandatory safety standards or information standards for consumer goods or product-related services
  2. banning consumer goods or product-related services, either on an interim or permanent basis
  3. issuing a compulsory recall notice requiring suppliers to recall consumer goods.
The provisions also regulate supplier responsibilities, including:
  1. what to do when a voluntary recall is initiated for safety related reasons
  2. supplier responsibilities relating to exports of certain goods
  3. when to report an incident associated with a consumer good to the Minister
  4. when a manufacturer may be liable for loss or damage caused by a product with a safety defect.

Product safety regulations

The safety of consumer products and product-related services is regulated through a harmonised, nationally consistent framework.

Mandatory safety and information standards

More details: Mandatory standards

Under the ACL, only the Commonwealth Minister has the power to make or declare a mandatory safety standard or information standard for a consumer product or product-related service. These standards apply nationally.

Interim and permanent bans

More details: product bans

The Commonwealth Minister and the relevant state or territory ministers have the power to impose an interim ban. Interim bans last for 60 days, and can be extended by a further 30 days on two separate occasions. Interim bans only apply in the state or territory in which they are imposed. Interim bans imposed by the Commonwealth Minister apply nationally.

The Commonwealth Minister has the power to impose a permanent ban on a consumer good or product-related service. Permanent bans apply nationally.

Company recall notices

The Commonwealth Minister and the relevant state or territory ministers can issue a compulsory recall notice for consumer goods. This can require the supplier (or if the supplier cannot be found, the state or territory regulator) to take certain action.

Public warning powers

Under the ACL, there are two ways to warn the public about certain matters:

  1. the Commonwealth Minister or the relevant state or territory ministers can issue a safety warning notice:
    1. stating that certain consumer goods or product related services are under investigation and/or
    2. advising of the risks involved in the use of the consumer goods or the supply of the product related services.
  2. the state and territory consumer protection agencies can issue public warning notices:
    1. when they suspect a contravention of the consumer protection provisions
    2. where it is in the public interest.
    3. where someone is likely to suffer detriment from the conduct

Redress for non-party comsumers

The ERECALL has the ability to represent multiple consumers and seek orders for redress for those consumers not party to the legal action. This empowers the ERECALL to deal more efficiently with matters that affect multiple consumers.

Mandatory reporting

More details: Mandatory reporting

Suppliers are required by law to advise the ERECALL when they become aware that the use or foreseeable misuse of a consumer good or product-related service they have supplied has caused or is associated with the death or serious injury or illness to any person. They are also required to report if another person (e.g. a customer) considers that the death, injury or illness was, or associated with, the use or foreseeable misuse of the consumer good or product-related service.
